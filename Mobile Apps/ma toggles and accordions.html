The lesson headers have that nice side by side effect going thanks to the "Columns" widget. Toggles are a fancy widget that allow you to hide away content behind a heading, so that people can find the content they're looking for faster. Here's one right here! Here's a picture of what the toggle shortcode looks like: ![image](http://labs.everettprogram.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Edit_Post_‹_Everett_Labs_—_WordPress.png) It produces this ![image](http://labs.everettprogram.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Everett_Labs_–_Toggles_and_Accordions.png)![image](http://labs.everettprogram.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Everett_Labs_–_Toggles_and_Accordions1.png)Simple as that! ## How Can I Implement This? It's really as simple as wrapping up whatever you want to be in a collapsible in the shortcode tags. Just make sure, like with HTML, that you have a opening and a closing. ## What's An Accordion? Oh right, that. An accordion is a consecutive set of toggles without any content in between the toggles. Only one can be open at once.
